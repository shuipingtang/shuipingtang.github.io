## 📑 Featured Research

### 📄 Dual Anchors: China-U.S. Factors Jointly Determining Global Gold and Crude Oil Pricing
*— Systematic Evidence Based on Exhaustive Testing with VIF Constraints*

This study employs a rolling regression framework with variance inflation factor (VIF) constraints to exhaustively test **462 factor combinations for gold** and **141 for crude oil**. The key findings:

- **🥇 Gold Optimal Model (R² = 0.908, rolling):** TIPS real yield + China-U.S. 10-year yield spread + gold-oil ratio. All VIFs below 5.
- **🛢️ Crude Oil Optimal Model (R² = 0.889):** Shanghai crude oil futures (SC) + VIX + synthetic time spread + China-U.S. yield spread. Out-of-sample R² = 0.611.
- **🔗 Cross-Market Common Factor:** The China-U.S. yield spread is the **only factor** entering both optimal models. Its pricing function in gold traces back to at least 2006; in crude oil, it was strictly activated by the launch of Shanghai crude oil futures in 2018.

## 🔬 Methodology

The research introduces a transparent, objective factor selection framework:

- **Exhaustive Testing:** All possible factor combinations are tested, allowing the optimal solution to emerge from the data rather than researcher preselection.
- **VIF Hard Constraints:** Variance inflation factor thresholds ensure factor independence and interpretability.
- **Multiple Robustness Checks:** Newey-West correction, LASSO regularization, permutation tests, and out-of-sample validation form a multi-layered verification system.
- **Quasi-Natural Experiment:** The 2018 launch of Shanghai crude oil futures provides a clean institutional breakpoint for causal identification.

This methodology is generalizable to any multi-factor asset pricing problem.
